--- name: i-stumbled-on-to-this-nice-piece-by layout: post title: What's my point of view? time: 2008-12-10 22:29:00 +05:30 --- I stumbled on to this nice piece by Duncan, and a follow up by bbum, about taking a different point of view while photographing something. Made me dig up my photos to see how many of them employ a somewhat unusual line of sight. And I have to say there weren't many.



Shot in IIMB during a discussion-ish session in Barcamp Bangalore 6

Self-portrait from inside an auto-rickshaw in Delhi


My poor old camera was placed face up on the road to get this, on self-timer and an uberlong exposure. This is the underbelly of the Eiffel Tower.


The drizzle brought out the umbrellas, and they always look good from the top. Shot from a rooftop corridor in the Notre-Dame de Paris.


Shot sitting in another rickshaw just in front of this one, this photo would have been impossible without the pull out LCD. Moradabad, India.


This probably looks like normal line of sight, but is shot from a low height to put the doggy in perspective. Antibes, France.



This one should really have been a simple shot, if only the scene had not been behind a grille. This is all I got out of poking my camera into a locked room in the Aurobindo Institute, Calcutta.
